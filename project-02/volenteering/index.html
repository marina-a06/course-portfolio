Treasurer

Parent Teacher Organization | 2024-Present

    Core Financial Duties

  • Maintain accurate and up-to-date financial records for all PTO income and expenses
  • Manage the PTO bank account(s), including deposits, withdrawals, and monthly reconciliation
  • Prepare and present monthly financial reports to the PTO board and general membership
  • Develop an annual budget in collaboration with the PTO board
  • Track spending against the approved budget and flag any discrepancies

    • Compliance & Reporting

  • Ensure compliance with PTO bylaws, district policies, and relevant financial regulations
  • Prepare year-end financial statements and assist with required audits or financial reviews
  • Maintain documentation for all transactions (receipts, invoices, reimbursement forms)
  • File necessary federal and state tax forms (often Form 990 series for nonprofits)
  • Protect the PTO’s nonprofit status by following required reporting rules

    • Funds Handling

  • Oversee cash handling procedures during events and fundraisers
  • Ensure two-person verification for cash counting to maintain transparency
  • Coordinate with fundraising chairs to track revenue from each event
  • Issue checks and handle reimbursements promptly

    • Communication & Collaboration

  • Work closely with the PTO president and board to plan financially responsible events and programs
  • Communicate budget updates and financial implications of proposed activities
  • Support committee leads in understanding spending guidelines and reimbursement processes
  • Present easy-to-understand financial summaries for parents and school staff

    • Recordkeeping & Transition

  • Maintain organized digital and paper files for all financial records
  • Provide a detailed, orderly treasurer’s binder or digital folder for smooth transitions
  • Train the incoming treasurer or assist during the hand-off period
